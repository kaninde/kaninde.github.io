# About Powerlifting Meet Strategy (PMS)

Welcome to **Powerlifting Meet Strategy (PMS)** — an app to plan and run powerlifting meets on your phone: tactical decisions in seconds, under pressure.

PMS helps you register **meets** and **athletes**, set **openers** and attempts, follow a **live** lifting order, and see **leaderboard** standings using rules you choose (for example federation-style totals and ranking). **Meet data**, **athlete profiles**, and **results** are stored **locally** on your device (SQLite). You can **export** and **import** a structured backup (ZIP with a manifest) from the Data area when that build includes it.

---

## Highlights

- **Meet hub:** Active meets, resume flow, archived history, and shortcuts into strategy screens.
- **New meet wizard:** Name, date, federation/rules, athlete, category and body weight, openers, and review.
- **Live & leaderboard:** Track attempts, lights/outcomes where the UI supports it, and ranking according to your configured formula.
- **Athlete profiles:** Training snapshot, personal records, coach confidence notes — all for your own meet prep, not a public profile service.
- **Settings:** App language, theme, default weight unit (kg/lb), ranking defaults, and optional haptic/visual alert preferences.
- **Privacy-first core:** Your meet and athlete data stay on the device unless you export a file or use features that need the internet (see the Privacy Policy).

## FAQ

**Do I need an account?**  
No. You can use PMS for the core local workflow without creating an account.

**Do I need internet?**  
Most meet planning and live use work **offline**. Internet is used when you choose features that need it (for example **ads**, **in-app purchases**, loading optional **remote configuration** such as an “other apps” carousel, or opening external links).

**Will I lose my data if I uninstall?**  
Local data lives on your device. Uninstalling the app can **permanently remove** that data unless you have exported a backup ZIP.

**Is this an official federation app?**  
No. PMS is a **tool for you and your coach**. You remain responsible for verifying weights, attempts, and rules against the official technical controller and competition regulations.
